    1. How can you use The ACID Test to identify this as bullying?

    A
    Aggressive - While we can’t read the text, it looks like the girls are texting Kimberly something mean or cruel to make fun of her and put her down.
    C
    Continual - While the text is instantaneous, cyberbullying is active and can happen 24/7 at any time or place. Kimberly receives her text during school.
    I
    Imbalance of power - Three girls appear to be ganging up on Kimberly. Because she is outnumbered three to one, this looks like a power imbalance.
    D
    Deliberate - The girls intentionally text Kimberly, showing that they are targeting her directly.

    Writing Sample
    I started going to a new high school and had to ride the bus to get there. Greg and his friends from the football team were these older kids and every time I sat down they told me to get up and threw their backpacks on all the empty seats. They pushed me down the aisle, and every time the bus driver screamed at me to sit down so she could drive.

    I didn’t know who to talk to, but I found myself telling my English teacher, Ms. Stevens. She told me that she could talk to Greg, that she knew him well. Then she said she would make a deal with me, if I started an anti-bullying campaign—shared stories with other kids of what happens to the whole group when we let kids bully other kids, asked them to help me write signs to post all over, and stand together whenever we see bullying. I did that and after my first year here, I was voted vice-president of the class.
